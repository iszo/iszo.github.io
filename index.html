Prince

Prince came to DACC after his owner had an accident with a hot pot of oil. The owner ended up in the hospital and, knowing he couldn't afford the vet bills that would come with Prince's injuries, asked us to take care of him. He's still on the mend, but has found a home ready to keep taking care of him.

Lizzy

Lizzy was a severely underweight neglect case who came in to DACC so weak and stressed that she stayed in the clinic full time until a foster took her home. That foster failed and adopted her - now she's living her best life with a huge yard, a pond, and regular trips on a boat.

Pixie

Pixie was found as a young puppy having been used to train fighting dogs. Covered in old bite wounds all over her face, neck, and paws, she was shut down at the shelter. All of the noise, other dogs, and strangers were so overwhelming she wouldn't move. After a few months in her new home, is ready to destroy any tennis ball she sees.

Our Mission:

Help Detroit's Animals

We work hand in hand with Detroit Animal Care and Control to help the homeless animals in our city. We also help DACC by doing all we can to keep animals out of the shelter in the first place.
